# Product Guide: OpenFisca Aotearoa

## Initial Concept
An OpenFisca Rules As Code country package for Aotearoa (New Zealand), providing computational models of the country's legislation, regulation, and government policy.

## Purpose of the Library & Aotearoa (NZ) Branch
This repository is the dedicated country package for New Zealand's legislation within the global OpenFisca ecosystem. 

Its primary purposes are:
1. **Codifying New Zealand Law:** Translating NZ legislation (such as the Social Security Act, Income Tax Act, and family support rules) into executable, testable python rules.
2. **Rules-as-Code (RaC) Implementation:** Serving as the backend calculation engine for civil society, government agencies, and developers to verify policy eligibility and simulate entitlements.
3. **Citizen and Civic Tech Collaboration:** Carrying forward the work initiated by the NZ Government's former Service Innovation Lab, Digital Aotearoa Collective, and Better Rules initiatives to make public policy transparent and accessible.

## Target Audience and Users
The computational rules and Web API served by OpenFisca Aotearoa target the following primary groups:
- **Government Agencies and Policy Analysts:** Who need to simulate policy changes, assess the impacts of legislative updates, and run automated calculations.
- **Software Developers and Third-Party Integrations:** Who build digital public services and consumer-facing applications that require direct calculation or validation of eligibility based on New Zealand legislation (e.g., SmartStart, family assistance calculators).
- **Citizen-led Initiatives and Researchers:** Who study and audit tax/benefit rules to ensure public transparency and analyze policy equity.

## Strategic Goals
1. **Legislation as Code:** Provide a single, machine-readable source of truth for New Zealand's complex legislative rules.
2. **Interoperability:** Deliver a high-performance Web API that can be easily integrated into any web application or microservice.
3. **Open Collaboration:** Foster transparency and citizen engagement by hosting an open-source, community-maintained rules repository.
4. **Accuracy & Quality:** Maintain a robust, test-driven suite of situation and integration tests to verify the accuracy of the modeled legislation.
